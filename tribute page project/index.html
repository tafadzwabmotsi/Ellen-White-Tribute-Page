Ellen G. White

A woman whose life has made impact the Seventh-Day Adent movement


Ellen G. White fellowshiping with brethren. Sitted in the middle and opening a book

Here is the inspirational life of Ellen G. White and her life time duties:

  • On November 26, 1827 - Ellen Gould Harmon is born in Gorham, Maine, U.S.

  • In 1836 - At the age of 9, a fatal accident that happens and could not continue with her formal education.

  • In 1843 - Ellen and her parents having been originally Methodists, are deprived of fellowship in the Methodist Church because of their sympathies with the Millerite movement and its adventist views.

  • In 1844 - Ellen begins experiencing seeing visions, and starts authoring her revelations.

  • On August 30, 1846 - At Portland, Maine, she marries Pastor James Springer White , a minister of the Christian Connection and an Adventist adherent.

  • In the 1860s - Mrs E. White plays a key role in forming the Seventh-Day Adventist Church and in developing its evangelistic outreach, working for the movement in the U.S.A.

  • In the years 1885-88 - Her ministrations attends the European region and her work furthers in this movement.

  • On August 8, 1894 - She is invited and received in Australia by the Church's foreign mission board. Despite severe attacks of rheumatism, she travelled throughout Tasmania, Victoria, South Australia, New South Wales, Queensland and New Zealand, attending Church meetings and giving public lectures to audiences sometimes numbering thousands.

  • In 1900 - Ellen White returns to America. During her ministry, her topics ranged from health reform and temperance to biblical interpretation and prophecy. Adventist Church leaders in Australia and abroad often sought her counsel.

  • Years later than 1900 - She continues teaching and writing. Her influence has led directly to the organization of Adventist day schools in Australia, and to the establishment of Avondale College and the Sydney Sanitarium (later the Sydney Adventist Hospital) at Wahroonga. She has also guided the development of the Church's administrative structure in Australia and encouraged the manufacture of health foods by the Adventist-owned Sanitarium Health Food Co.

  • On July 16, 1915 - Ellen White dies in Elmshaven, St. Helena, California, U.S.
